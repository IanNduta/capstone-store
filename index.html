What is a Tensegrity

Tensegrity structures, characterized by a balance between tension and compression elements, defy conventional architectural norms. These innovative frameworks consist of a network of struts and cables, where the tension in the cables maintains the integrity of the entire system by pulling against the compression of the struts. This design principle, championed by visionaries like Buckminster Fuller, offers a remarkable blend of strength, flexibility, and aesthetic appeal, inspiring applications ranging from art installations to engineering marvels.

In biology, tensegrity structures also find a parallel, notably in cellular architecture. The cytoskeleton of cells, composed of microfilaments and microtubules suspended in a tensegrity-like arrangement, provides structural support while allowing for dynamic cellular processes like shape changes and movement. This biomimetic approach not only informs architectural design but also underscores the fundamental principles of balance and resilience found throughout nature.
