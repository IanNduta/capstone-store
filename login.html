What is a Tensegrity

The characteristic property of a stable three-dimensional structure consisting of members under tension that are contiguous and members under compression that are not.

The tensegrity concept has found applications within architecture and civil engineering, such as towers, large dome structures, stadium roofs, temporarily structures and tents. Towers which composed of interconnected tensegrity modules are the best known tensegrity structures.
